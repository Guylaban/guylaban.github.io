01 — About

Human connection, artificial partners

I am a Senior Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in the Department of Industrial Engineering & Management at Ben-Gurion University of the Negev. My research sits at the intersection of human-robot interaction, affective computing and health psychology — asking how, when and why people open up to machines.

Previously I was a Research Associate in the Affective Intelligence & Robotics Laboratory (AFAR) at the University of Cambridge's Department of Computer Science & Technology, led by Prof. Hatice Gunes. I completed my PhD at the University of Glasgow under Prof. Emily S. Cross (Glasgow & ETH Zürich) and Prof. Val Morrison (Bangor), as an ESR in the Marie Skłodowska-Curie network ENTWINE.

Off the clock you'll find me in the mountains — hiking and snowboarding — or behind a camera.

What I work on

Human-Robot Interaction
